President's Welcome Letter

Welcome to Hankerson University: The Real HU, where acceptance is based not on grades, but on bloodlines and chosen bonds.

Dear Family,

Congratulations.

We are proud to inform you that you have been officially accepted to Hankerson University: The Real HU. Your acceptance is not based on grades, test scores, or legacy status. It is based on something older and stronger: bloodlines and chosen bonds, shared history, and a love that has carried us through generations, through seasons and through change.

In 1978, the Hankerson family made a decision to gather on purpose. What began as an idea became an annual reunion tradition. What became a tradition grew into a family institution. This year, on July 17–19, we return as students and teachers of that legacy.

Hankerson University exists to do three things:

  • 1Honor those who came before us
  • 2Invest in those who are here now
  • 3Prepare those who are coming next

Your presence matters. Your stories matter. Your laughter counts as participation.

With love,
The Sister-Cousin Connection

Reunion Founders

Hankerson University, The Real HU, traces its very first gathering to 1978 in Tampa, Florida, founded and hosted by Gwen Porter. What she began as a single afternoon of family fellowship has now carried the Hankerson name across 48 summers and dozens of cities.

This year's reunion is hosted by the Sister-Cousin Connection: Shantae Bolden, Tara Deloach, Anita Gibson, Keishon Graham, Sharmaine Lewis, Lashauna Bolden Morris, and Latoyor Owens.

Hall of Legacy · To the Oldest Male and Female

I thought upon the elderly and whispered a prayer, giving thanks to God for their sojourn here. Such kind and gentle souls from generations ago, what they are to us, they will never know.

A sure and beaten path; a guide along the way. The dedicated laborer who's now old and gray. Patriarchs and matriarchs, and early pioneers. The bridge that has brought us as we've journeyed here.

Soldiers of great sacrifice, treasured more than art. We honor, love and cherish them deep within our heart.

~ Walterrean Salley (Savannah, GA)

Family Anthem · Lift Every Voice and Sing

Lift every voice and sing,

Till earth and heaven ring,

Ring with the harmonies of Liberty;

Let our rejoicing rise

High as the listening skies,

Let it resound loud as the rolling sea.

Sing a song full of the faith that the dark past has taught us,

Sing a song full of the hope that the present has brought us;

Facing the rising sun of our new day begun,

Let us march on till victory is won.

by James Weldon Johnson (1900)

Black Family Pledge

Because we have forgotten our ancestors, our children no longer give us honor. Because we have lost the path our ancestors cleared kneeling in perilous undergrowth, our children cannot find their way.

Therefore, we pledge to bind ourselves to one another: to embrace our lowliest, to keep company with our loneliest, to educate our illiterate, to feed our starving, to clothe our ragged. To do all things, knowing that we are more than keepers of our brothers and sisters. We are brothers and sisters.

~ Dr. Maya Angelou, May 14, 1986
